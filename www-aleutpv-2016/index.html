Harbor Seal Telemetry Deployments

This website provides updated summaries and display graphics for satellite tags deployed on harbor seals during the 2016 Aleutian harbor seal research cruise. Two external satellite telemetry devices were adhered to the hair of the animals in order to provide movement and dive behavior.

The table below provides a direct link to auto-generated reports for each seal ('speno'). A more comprehensive table of deployment and seal information is also available

SPLASH10 Tags

SPLASH10 model tags were adhered to the hair of the harbor seal on either the head or the back. For many of the tags deployed during this project, both Argos-based locations and GPS-based locations are available. GPS locations have an error of +/- 50m. Argos locations can have significantly larger error.

SPOT6 Flipper Tags

Flipper tags based on the SPOT6 platform were attached to the webbing of the rear flipper for most seals. These tags provide course location and hourly haul-out behavior and are programmed to last up to 2 years.
